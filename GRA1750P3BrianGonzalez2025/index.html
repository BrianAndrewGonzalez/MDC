Cuban Picadillo Enpanada

A Cuban picadillo empanada is a savory pastry filled with picadillo, a traditional Cuban ground beef mixture. The filling usually includes seasoned ground beef, tomato sauce, onions, garlic, green olives, and sometimes raisins or potatoes for a sweet-savory twist. The mixture is wrapped in empanada dough and then baked or fried until golden and crispy. Its a popular snack or appetizer in Cuban cuisine, known for its rich flavor and satisfying texture.

Ingredients (for about 12 empanadas)

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • ½ green bell pepper, finely chopped
  • 1 pound ground beef (80/20 works well)
  • ½ cup tomato sauce
  • ¼ cup pimento-stuffed green olives, sliced
  • 1 tablespoon capers (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons raisins (optional for sweetness)
  • ½ teaspoon ground cumin
  • ½ teaspoon oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 (14 oz) package of empanada dough discs (or use pie dough cut into 5-inch circles)
  • 1 egg (beaten, for egg wash)
  • Oil for frying (or baking spray if baking)

Steps

  1. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Sauté onion, bell pepper, and garlic until softened (about 3-4 minutes).
  2. Add ground beef and cook until browned, breaking it up as it cooks (5-7 minutes).
  3. Stir in tomato sauce, green olives, raisins, capers (if using), cumin, oregano, salt, and pepper. Let the mixture simmer on low for 8–10 minutes, then remove from heat and let cool slightly.
  4. Lay out empanada discs and place about 1–2 tablespoons of the cooled picadillo filling in the center of each.
  5. Fold and seal the empanadas by folding in half and pressing the edges with a fork. Brush tops with beaten egg if baking.
  6. To fry: Heat oil in a pan to 350°F (175°C) and fry empanadas until golden and crispy, about 2–3 minutes per side.
  7. Let cool slightly, then serve warm. Enjoy!
